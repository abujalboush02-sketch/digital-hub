PART 1: SOURCING ASSETS THE BUY SIDE
SWIPE 01 // THE ABANDONED REPO Target: Inactive GitHub with commercial SaaS tier
Subject: Question about [Project Name]
Hey [Name], I was looking through the GitHub repo for [Project Name] today. Really elegant way you solved [Specific Technical Problem]. I noticed there haven't been any commits in a while and the main site looks a bit quiet. Are you still actively working on this, or have you moved on to other projects? I run a small portfolio of micro-tools and specialize in taking over projects like this to keep the servers running for existing users. If you're looking to offload it, I'd be happy to make a fast, cash offer to take it off your hands this week. Best, [Your Name]

Technical Flattery

By mentioning a "Specific Technical Problem," you prove you aren't a bot. Developers are more likely to respond to someone who respects the engineering work.

SWIPE 02 // THE "SHUTTING DOWN" POST Target: Post-mortem announcements
Subject: Don't shut down [App Name] just yet!
Hey [Name], Just read your post on [IndieHackers/Reddit]. Sorry to hear you're shutting down [App Name]. Building is a grind, and I respect the decision to move on. Before you turn off the servers, would you be open to selling the assets? I buy small SaaS projects specifically to save them from being shut down. I can offer a quick cash buyout ($[Amount]) for the code, domain, and user list. It gives you a small exit win and ensures your users don't lose the tool. Let me know if you're open to a quick chat. Best, [Your Name]

The Anchor Price Strategy

When calculating the $[Amount] for Swipe 02, use these operational brackets:

The "Bail Out" ($500 - $2,000)

Best for apps with 0 revenue but established codebases and 100+ "dead" users.

The "Multiple" (12x - 24x MRR)

For assets with proven revenue. Always anchor at the lower multiple (12x) for cold outreach.

PART 2: CUSTOMER ACQUISITION THE SELL SIDE
SWIPE 03 // THE B2B OFFER Target: ICP LinkedIn/Cold Email (Week 3 Sprint)
Subject: Quick question about your [Process]
Hey [Name], Saw that [Company Name] is scaling up your [Relevant Department/Action]. I recently took over a tool called [App Name]. It completely automates [Specific Pain Point]. We just revamped the entire platform and I'm looking for a few sharp operators to test it out. I'm not trying to sell you a subscription today. I'd love to give you a completely free Pro account for the next month. In exchange, all I ask is 2 sentences of feedback on how we can improve it. Mind if I send over the access link? Best, [Your Name]

Low-Friction Conversions

"I'm not trying to sell you a subscription today" is the most powerful sentence in cold outreach. It lowers friction and frames the interaction as a professional exchange of value rather than a pitch.

Subject: A new tool for your readers (and a free upgrade)
Hey [Name], I've been a subscriber to [Newsletter Name] for a few months—loved the recent piece on [Topic]. I run a micro-SaaS called [App Name] that helps [Target Audience] do [Benefit]. Since your audience is exactly who we built this for, I'd love to offer your readers an exclusive perk. If you think it's a fit for an upcoming issue, I can generate a promo code ([NEWSLETTERNAME]20) that gives your readers 20% off for life. Let me know if you'd like a free account to play around with it first! Best, [Your Name]

The Directory Hunt Strategy

To maximize the reach of Swipe 04, target curators on these platforms:

  • Substack Search: Find newsletters with 1,000 - 5,000 subscribers. They are hungry for high-value tools to share.
  • Toolify.ai / BetaList: Reach out to the owners of niche directories to ask for a "Featured Spot" using the free account offer as a hook.
